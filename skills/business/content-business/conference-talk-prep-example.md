# Conference Talk Prep — Example

## Scenario: Building a 30-Minute Agentic ERP Session for a Dynamics Conference

**Who:** Ragnar Pitla, Principal PM, Microsoft Agentic Team
**Where:** Accepted to speak at Directions EMEA; 30-minute session slot; audience is Dynamics 365 partners and customers, mixed technical and business background
**The Problem:** The session title is confirmed ("Agentic ERP: What It Means for Your D365 Investment") but the structure, key talking points, and demo plan are blank. The conference is 6 weeks out. The session needs to land with both the business decision-makers in the room and the technical architects who will implement what they hear. One demo required. No slides yet.

**Prerequisites:** Session title and time slot confirmed; demo environment with D365 F&O and Copilot Studio available.

---

### What You Say to the Agent

> "Help me structure a 30-minute conference session on Agentic ERP for Directions EMEA. Audience is D365 partners and customers, mixed business and technical. I need a structure, key talking points for each section, and a demo plan. The core message I want to land: ERP systems are about to get a reasoning layer, and the partners who understand this first will win the next wave of implementations."

### What the Agent Does

1. Builds the 30-minute structure with tight timing:
   - 0-3 min: The hook. Open with a real number that reframes the room ("The average D365 user makes 47 manual decisions per day that the system already has the data to make for them. Agentic ERP closes that gap.")
   - 3-10 min: What agentic ERP is NOT (not more automation, not AI bolt-on, not replacing D365) versus what it IS (a reasoning layer that participates in business processes)
   - 10-18 min: The architecture. Copilot Studio + D365 F&O + MCP + Dataverse policy tables. One slide, one diagram, plain language. Show where each component lives.
   - 18-25 min: Live demo. Materials shortage scenario — show the agent detecting a production order at risk, surfacing the recommended action to the planner, and waiting for approval before acting in D365.
   - 25-28 min: The partner opportunity. What this means for implementation projects, what skills to build now.
   - 28-30 min: Q&A setup with the one question you want them to leave asking ("Which of your customers' processes would they trust an agent to handle?")

2. Writes the key talking points for the architecture section, including the one-sentence description of each component that both business and technical audiences can follow

3. Scripts the demo narration — what to say while the demo runs, what to highlight, and what to say if it breaks ("I'll show you the output we captured this morning because live demos and ERP systems have a complicated relationship")

4. Recommends 3 backup slides in case the demo environment is unavailable: screenshot walkthrough of the same scenario with annotated callouts

### The Business Impact

- **Session fully structured in 2 hours** instead of a week of outline drafts
- **Demo plan de-risked** with a backup path built in before rehearsal
- **Both audiences served** — business attendees get the "why," architects get the component diagram
- **Talk rated in top 10% of sessions** at the conference; 3 partner firms reach out after for project conversations

### Try It Yourself

> "I'm speaking at a local Power Platform user group for 45 minutes on Copilot Studio multi-agent architecture. The audience is mostly developers. Help me structure the session and pick a demo that shows the value of connected agents versus a single agent."

# Case Study Generator — Example

## Scenario: Turning a Copilot Studio D365 Deployment Win into a Case Study

**Who:** Tom Bellamy, Practice Director, CloudPath Solutions (Microsoft partner, 120 employees)
**Where:** Marketing initiative to build the firm's case study library; just closed a successful Copilot Studio + D365 deployment for a UK-based retail chain
**The Problem:** The engagement was a clear win — the client reduced manual purchase order approvals by 73% — but Tom has raw notes from the project, no story structure, and a marketing team waiting for content. The notes include a Slack thread, a few bullet points from the closeout meeting, and a before/after metric summary. No polished narrative. The website case study slot has been empty for 4 months.

**Prerequisites:** Raw project notes, client approval to publish (obtained), key metrics from the engagement.

---

### What You Say to the Agent

> "Help me turn these project notes into a case study for our website. Client is a 340-store UK retail chain running D365 F&O. We built a Copilot Studio agent for their AP team to handle purchase order approvals. Before: approvals took 3-5 days, 2 AP staff processing 400+ POs per week manually. After: 73% of POs auto-approved by the agent within 15 minutes, AP staff redirected to exception handling and vendor relationship work. The client's AP Manager said 'it feels like we finally have a system that works with us, not against us.' Here are my raw notes: [notes pasted in]"

### What the Agent Does

1. Identifies the story structure from the raw notes: the hook (retail chain growing faster than their AP team could keep up), the stakes (delayed POs causing stock-outs in 18 stores during peak season), the solution (Copilot Studio agent with policy-driven approval logic in Dataverse), and the outcome (numbers + human quote)

2. Drafts the case study in 5 sections: Challenge, Approach, Solution Architecture (plain-English, no jargon), Results, and What the Client Said — each section calibrated for a business decision-maker reading the partner's website, not a technical audience

3. Extracts the 3 headline metrics and formats them for the visual callout boxes that websites typically use: "73% of POs approved automatically," "15 minutes vs. 3-5 days approval cycle," "2 AP staff redeployed to higher-value work"

4. Writes a 2-sentence SEO summary for the page meta description targeting "Copilot Studio D365 accounts payable automation"

5. Flags 2 gaps in the raw notes that need client confirmation before publishing: the exact number of stores affected by stock-outs during peak season (makes the stakes concrete), and whether the client approves naming the retail chain or prefers "a leading UK retail chain"

### The Business Impact

- **Case study published in one week** instead of sitting on the to-do list for another quarter
- **3 inbound leads in 30 days** from prospects who found the case study searching for Copilot Studio AP automation
- **Sales team uses it in 11 deals** as proof of ROI for similar AP improvement conversations
- **Client relationship strengthened** — the AP Manager was quoted by name and shared the case study on LinkedIn

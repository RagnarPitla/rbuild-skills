# Outlook Newsletter — Example

## Scenario: Internal AI Strategy Newsletter for 800 Employees

**Who:** Dana Osei, Head of Digital Transformation, global manufacturing company (800 employees)
**Where:** Microsoft 365, Outlook with Newsletters enabled
**The Problem:** Dana sends a monthly "AI at Work" update via a shared mailbox. Half the team never opens it, there are no analytics, replies go to the wrong inbox, and she has no idea if anyone reads the links she shares. She wants a proper newsletter setup with tracking and a subscriber list she can manage.

**Prerequisites:** Microsoft 365 account with Newsletters feature enabled by your M365 admin

---

### What You Say to the Agent

> "Help me set up an Outlook newsletter called 'AI at Work' for our 800-person company. It should be visible to everyone in the org, have a clean header image, and auto-subscribe everyone by default. Walk me through the setup and what I should put in the first edition."

### What the Agent Does

1. Walks through the creation steps: title "AI at Work", description "Monthly digest of AI tools, experiments, and wins from across the business", visibility set to "My organization", auto-subscribe toggle on so all 800 employees are subscribed from day one
2. Specifies the header image requirements (704px wide, 16:9) and suggests using the image-prompt-generator skill to create a clean M365-branded header in Lovart or Canva
3. Drafts a first edition structure: hook (one AI win from the business this month), 3 short items (tool spotlight, experiment result, resource link), and a simple CTA ("Reply to Dana with your AI question of the month")

### The Business Impact

- **Open rate visibility for the first time** — Dana discovers the first edition gets a 41% open rate, far above the old shared mailbox average she could never measure
- **Zero inbox chaos** — replies no longer land in a shared mailbox, subscriber management is centralized
- **Subscriber list exportable** — Dana exports the list to use for a future Teams channel invite and town hall registration

# YouTube Script — Example

## Scenario: Turning a Dry Technical Concept into a 12-Minute Hit

**Who:** Ragnar Pitla (via his YouTube channel @RagnarPitla), content for AI practitioners and enterprise professionals
**Where:** YouTube — tech thought leadership content
**The Problem:** Ragnar needs to explain Model Context Protocol (MCP) to a business audience — people who aren't developers but need to understand why their IT teams are asking for 6 weeks to "connect AI to D365." The concept is technically complex. A dry explainer will get 200 views. A well-structured hook-and-shift script will get 20,000.

**Prerequisites:** A clear topic and core insight to communicate. The script structure works for 8-20 minute videos.

---

### What You Say to the Agent

> "Write a YouTube script for a 12-minute video explaining MCP (Model Context Protocol) to business leaders and enterprise professionals. The hook should challenge the assumption that 'AI just connects to anything.' Use my voice — confident practitioner, framework-driven, grounded in real builds. Include B-roll cues."

### What the Agent Does

Produces a complete script:

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:45]
[CAMERA — direct address]
Your IT team just told you connecting AI to your ERP is a 6-week project.
And you're thinking — it's just a chatbot. Why does it take six weeks?

Here's the real answer. And it's actually good news.

[B-ROLL: Split screen — simple chat interface on left, complex system 
diagram on right]

The AI part? That takes about 20 minutes.
The other 5 weeks and 6 days? That's building something you'll use
for every AI project for the next decade.

That thing is called MCP. And by the end of this video, you'll understand
exactly why it matters — and how to use that knowledge to make smarter
decisions about your AI strategy.

[SHIFT 1: WHY THIS MATTERS — 0:45-5:00]
[CAMERA]
Before 2024, connecting an AI system to your ERP required...
```

The script includes all three shifts, B-roll cues, a demo section, and a CTA.

### The Business Impact

- **The MCP explainer video** using this script format got 34,000 views in the first month
- **Comments showed the hook worked:** "Finally an explanation that doesn't make me feel stupid" appeared in multiple variations
- **Direct business outcome:** Three enterprise leads reached out through the video, two became consulting engagements

# Email Writer

Staring at an email you need to send but don't know how to start? Tell Claude what you need to say and who you're saying it to. You'll have a draft in 30 seconds.

## How to Use

Just describe the situation in plain language:

```
Write an email to my client Sarah. She's been unresponsive for 
2 weeks after I sent a proposal. I want to follow up without 
being pushy. Keep it short. I need a response by Friday.
```

Or paste a draft for improvement:
```
Here's my draft email — it's too long and aggressive. 
Rewrite it to be firm but professional:
[paste draft]
```

## Email Types This Handles

**Follow-up emails** — after meetings, proposals, applications, introductions

**Cold outreach** — introducing yourself, pitching an idea, asking for a call

**Difficult messages** — delivering bad news, saying no, addressing a conflict

**Requests** — asking for something, getting approval, scheduling meetings

**Thank you / recognition** — genuine, not sycophantic

**Updates / status reports** — keeping stakeholders informed efficiently

**Negotiations** — counter-offers, pushing back, asking for more time

## Tell Claude These Things

For the best result, include:
- **Who you're writing to** (their role, your relationship)
- **What you want to happen** (what action you need from them)
- **The tone** (formal / professional / casual / warm)
- **The urgency** (ASAP / by [date] / no rush)
- **Any constraints** (must be under 100 words / can't mention X)

## Trigger Phrases

- "Write an email to [person] about [topic]"
- "Draft a follow-up email to [person]"
- "Help me say [message] in a professional email"
- "Rewrite this email: [paste email]"
- "Cold outreach email to [type of person] about [topic]"
- "Email to decline [invitation/request]"

## Example Prompts

```
"Email to my manager asking for a day off next Friday. 
I have personal reasons but don't want to share them. 
Professional but not stiff. Under 4 sentences."

"Cold email to a VP of IT at a manufacturing company. 
I'm selling an AI consulting service. I want to ask for 
a 15-minute call. Make it specific and not salesy."

"Email declining a meeting request from a vendor. 
I'm not interested in their product but want to keep 
the relationship open. Polite but final."
```

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Too formal / stiff | No tone specified | Add "conversational" or "like a real person" |
| Too long | No length constraint | Add "under 100 words" or "3 sentences max" |
| Wrong angle | Context incomplete | Add the history: "We met at X, they previously said Y" |

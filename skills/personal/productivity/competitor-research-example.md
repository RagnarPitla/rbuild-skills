# Competitor Research: Example

## Scenario: Sales Executive Uses a Competitor Brief to Win a Demo on Compliance Depth

**Who:** Elena Sousa, Enterprise Account Executive at a cloud compliance management software company. She manages mid-market and enterprise accounts. Deals typically close in 90-120 days with multiple stakeholders involved.
**Where:** Pre-demo research session in Claude, 2 hours before a discovery call
**The Problem:** Elena has a demo with a prospect at a financial services firm. The prospect mentioned they are also evaluating a direct competitor. Elena knows the competitor's name and has seen their website before, but she doesn't know their weaknesses, their pricing model, or what their customers actually say about them. She has 2 hours and no time to spend an hour clicking through review sites and LinkedIn.

**Prerequisites:** None beyond knowing the competitor's name. Exa MCP configured for live research (optional but used in this example).

---

### What You Say to the Agent

> "I have a demo in 2 hours with a financial services prospect. They're also evaluating [Competitor]. Build me a competitor brief. I need to know their positioning, what customers complain about, their pricing if known, and where I can differentiate. I sell compliance management software to mid-market financial services companies. My strengths are SOX audit readiness and automated evidence collection."

### What the Agent Does

1. **Pulls the company overview.** Founded 2018, Series B ($24M raised), 180 employees. Targets SMB and lower mid-market. Positions as "compliance made simple" with a self-serve onboarding model. Primary verticals: tech startups and e-commerce.

2. **Identifies the positioning gap immediately.** The competitor's "compliance made simple" positioning is aimed at companies with low compliance complexity. Financial services firms have high complexity. This is the first differentiation signal.

3. **Surfaces three strengths with evidence.** Strong G2 rating (4.4/5), praised for clean UI and fast setup. Customers consistently mention "up and running in a day." Strong integrations with AWS and Google Cloud. Good fit for companies that need basic SOC 2 Type II.

4. **Surfaces three weaknesses from customer reviews.** The recurring complaints on G2 and Capterra: "doesn't support financial services specific frameworks (SOX, PCI-DSS)," "evidence collection still requires manual exports," "support response times drop significantly for enterprise tier customers." One reviewer: "Great for startups, not ready for our audit requirements."

5. **Reads the job postings.** Elena asks Claude to check what they're hiring for. Three open roles: two compliance framework specialists and one enterprise sales manager. This signals they're aware of the gap and working toward it, but likely 6-12 months away from being enterprise-ready for financial services.

6. **Writes the battle card.** If the prospect brings up the competitor: acknowledge they have a clean product and fast onboarding, then pivot to the specific requirements of a financial services SOX audit. Elena's platform has pre-built SOX control templates and automated evidence collection. The competitor does not. For a financial services firm, that gap is the decision.

### The Business Impact

- **Elena leads the demo with compliance depth.** She opens with "I know you're also looking at [Competitor]. They do a great job for tech startups. For a financial services firm preparing for a SOX audit, here's why the requirements are different." The prospect's VP of Risk says "that's exactly what we've been trying to explain to our CFO."
- **Competitor is eliminated from consideration** at the end of the call. The prospect says the compliance framework coverage gap is a non-starter for them.
- **Deal advances to a proof of concept** within 48 hours. Closed 74 days later.
- **Elena builds a standard pre-call process.** For every demo, she now runs a 10-minute competitor brief 2 hours before the call. She says it's the highest-leverage 10 minutes in her week.

### Try It Yourself

> "Now build me a one-page battle card I can use with my full sales team. It should cover our three main competitors in the compliance management space, one key differentiator against each, and the 2 things you say when a prospect brings up each one. Keep it scannable, I want the team to be able to reference it during a call."

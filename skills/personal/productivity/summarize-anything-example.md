# Summarize Anything: Example

## Scenario: VP at a Logistics Firm Gets a 47-Page Industry Report Down to 6 Bullets and 2 Action Items

**Who:** Ahmed Al-Farsi, VP of Operations at a regional logistics and freight company. Oversees a team of 85, responsible for carrier management, warehouse operations, and technology adoption. Gets forwarded industry reports, analyst briefings, and whitepapers regularly. Has been skipping most of them for a year.
**Where:** Claude, 8 minutes before a leadership team meeting
**The Problem:** Ahmed's CEO forwarded a 47-page Gartner report on supply chain technology trends with the note "worth reading before Thursday's planning meeting." It's Wednesday. Ahmed has 8 minutes and needs to know if anything in the report is relevant to decisions his team is making about warehouse automation and carrier contracts. He is not going to read 47 pages. He never was.

**Prerequisites:** Access to the report (or its executive summary and key data tables). PDFs can be pasted as text.

---

### What You Say to the Agent

> "Summarize this industry report for me. I'm the VP of Operations at a logistics company. We're making decisions about warehouse automation investment and whether to renegotiate our carrier contracts this year. Give me a 6-bullet executive briefing focused only on what's relevant to those two decisions. End with any direct action items for my team. Here's the executive summary and the technology chapter: [pasted text]"

### What the Agent Does

1. **Reads the report with Ahmed's decision lens.** Not a general summary. Claude identifies which sections are about warehouse technology and which are about carrier relationships. Skips the retail-focused sections entirely because they're not relevant.

2. **Produces a 6-bullet executive briefing.** Each bullet is a complete decision-relevant finding:
   - Warehouse automation ROI breakeven has dropped from 4.2 years to 2.6 years since 2023, driven by lower hardware costs
   - 68% of logistics firms surveyed plan to automate pick-and-pack operations by 2027 (up from 31% in 2022)
   - Carrier capacity is tight in Q3-Q4 across the report's survey region, making contract locks advantageous this quarter
   - AI-based route optimization shows 12-18% fuel cost reduction in pilots across 5 companies profiled
   - Labor shortages in warehouse operations are projected to persist for 2-3 years, strengthening the automation business case
   - Early movers in automation are reporting 23% improvement in order accuracy, creating a customer retention advantage

3. **Identifies 2 direct action items.** Based on the two decisions Ahmed named: (1) The automation ROI case is stronger than last year's analysis assumed, worth a revised business case before the Thursday meeting. (2) Carrier contracts: the report signals Q3-Q4 tightness, which means now is the optimal window to negotiate multi-year rates, not after summer.

4. **Adds the "so what" conclusion.** "Given your two decisions: the data supports moving forward with the automation business case now rather than deferring to next year. On carrier contracts, the report suggests a short negotiating window this quarter. If both decisions are on Thursday's agenda, these two data points are worth putting in front of the leadership team."

### The Business Impact

- **Reading time: 3 minutes instead of 90 minutes.** Ahmed read the 6 bullets on his phone walking to the meeting.
- **Direct impact on the Thursday meeting.** Ahmed opened with the ROI and carrier timing data. The leadership team approved starting the automation business case. Carrier renegotiation was added to Q2 priorities.
- **Report now actually used.** Ahmed's team started sending summaries before forwarding reports. The CEO noticed that meeting preparation improved noticeably over the next two months.
- **Behavior shift.** Ahmed now summarizes every report before deciding whether to read the full thing. His rule: if the summary has something actionable, he reads the relevant chapter. Otherwise, the summary is enough.

### Try It Yourself

> "I just got out of a 90-minute strategy meeting and there's no recording, just a messy set of notes I took on my phone. Summarize these notes: extract the three decisions that were made, the open questions that still need answers, and any action items with owners. Tell me what I need to follow up on before next week."

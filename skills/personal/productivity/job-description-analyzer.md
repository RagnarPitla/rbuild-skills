# Job Description Analyzer

Reading job descriptions is exhausting. Should you apply? What will they actually care about? What gaps do you need to address?

This skill gives you an honest, fast evaluation — not the encouragement you want, but the clarity you need.

## How to Use

```
Analyze this job description against my background.

JOB DESCRIPTION:
[paste full JD]

MY BACKGROUND:
[paste your resume, LinkedIn summary, or just describe your experience]

TELL ME:
- Should I apply (honest fit score)?
- What are my strongest matches?
- What are my real gaps?
- What should I emphasize in my cover letter?
- What questions will they likely ask that I should prep for?
```

## What It Produces

### Fit Assessment
```
FIT SCORE: 7.5/10 — Strong candidate, apply

STRONG MATCHES
✅ [Requirement] — you have [evidence from your background]
✅ [Requirement] — you have [evidence]
✅ [Requirement] — you have [evidence]

GAPS TO ADDRESS
⚠ [Requirement] — you have partial experience / none
  → How to frame it: [suggested angle]
⚠ [Requirement] — missing
  → Whether to mention: [advice]

COVER LETTER FOCUS
Lead with: [strongest point]
Address: [gap that needs proactive acknowledgment]
Close with: [what makes you distinctive for this role]

LIKELY INTERVIEW QUESTIONS
1. [Question based on JD] — [prep angle]
2. [Question] — [prep angle]
3. [Question] — [prep angle]

RECOMMENDATION
Apply / Don't apply / Apply but tailor carefully — [1-sentence reason]
```

## Trigger Phrases

- "Analyze this job description for me: [paste JD]"
- "Should I apply for this role? [paste JD + background]"
- "Job fit analysis — [paste JD]"
- "What are my gaps for this role?"
- "Help me evaluate this job opportunity"

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Score seems off | Background not detailed enough | Add specific years of experience and key projects |
| Gaps feel inflated | JD uses unusual terminology | Ask "Is [term] the same as [what you have]?" |
| Generic interview questions | JD too vague | Ask for questions "based on this company's known interview style" |

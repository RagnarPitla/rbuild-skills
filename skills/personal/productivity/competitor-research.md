# Competitor Research

Before a sales call, pitch, or product decision — know exactly what you're up against. This skill builds a competitor brief you can actually use, not a generic Wikipedia summary.

## What You Give It

```
Research [Company/Product Name]. I'm a [your role/company] 
competing for [customer type]. I need to understand: 
their positioning, pricing (if known), key strengths, 
common weaknesses customers complain about, and where 
I can differentiate.
```

## What It Produces

### Competitor Profile
```
COMPETITOR: [Company Name]
Founded: [Year] | Size: [Employees] | Stage: [Funding/Public]

POSITIONING
How they describe themselves: "[their tagline/pitch]"
Who they target: [ideal customer profile]
Core value prop: [what they claim to do best]

PRICING (if known)
[Pricing tiers and what's included]

KEY STRENGTHS
✅ [Strength 1 — with evidence]
✅ [Strength 2]
✅ [Strength 3]

KNOWN WEAKNESSES
⚠ [Weakness 1 — what customers complain about]
⚠ [Weakness 2]
⚠ [Weakness 3]

WHERE YOU CAN WIN
→ [Differentiation opportunity 1]
→ [Differentiation opportunity 2]

WHAT THEIR CUSTOMERS SAY
[G2/Trustpilot/Reddit patterns if available]

BATTLE CARD SUMMARY
If a prospect mentions them: [what to say]
If they ask to compare: [key differentiators to emphasize]
```

## Use Cases

**Sales prep** — before a demo, know their objections before they raise them

**Product planning** — find gaps in their offering to build into

**Pricing decisions** — understand the market before setting your price

**Partnership evaluation** — due diligence before working with someone

**Job interview prep** — research a potential employer or their competitors

## Trigger Phrases

- "Research [company name] as a competitor"
- "Build a competitor brief for [company]"
- "What do I need to know about [company] before my call?"
- "Compare [Company A] vs [Company B]"
- "Competitive analysis of [market/product category]"
- "Battle card for [competitor]"

## With Exa MCP (Live Research)

When Exa MCP is configured, this skill searches the current web for:
- Recent product announcements
- Customer reviews (G2, Trustpilot, Reddit)
- Press coverage and funding news
- Job postings (reveals their priorities)
- LinkedIn data (team size, growth)

Without Exa, it works from training knowledge — best for established companies with public data.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Too surface level | Didn't specify your angle | Add "I'm competing for [customer type] on [criteria]" |
| Missing pricing | Competitor hides pricing | Ask "What do customers say about their pricing on review sites?" |
| No differentiation ideas | Missing your context | Add "We do [X] differently by [Y]" |
